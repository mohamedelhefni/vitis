## What is a Digital Garden?

A **digital garden** is a personal space for cultivating ideas, thoughts, and knowledge over time. Unlike traditional blogs with chronological posts, digital gardens embrace:

- **Non-linear exploration** - Content connects through ideas, not dates
- **Continuous growth** - Pages evolve and improve over time  
- **Interconnected knowledge** - Ideas link together naturally
- **Learning in public** - Share works-in-progress, not just finished pieces

## Why Vitis is Perfect for Digital Gardening

The Vitis theme brings powerful features that make digital gardening natural and enjoyable:

### 1. Wiki-Style Links

Create connections using simple `[[Page Title]]` syntax. Vitis automatically:
- Generates proper links between pages
- Shows [[backlinks]] on each page
- Highlights broken links to encourage content creation

### 2. Knowledge Graph Visualization

The built-in [knowledge graph](/graph) helps you:
- Visualize how your ideas connect
- Discover unexpected relationships
- Identify content clusters and gaps
- Navigate your garden visually

### 3. Series Organization

Group related posts into [[educational series]] with:
- Automatic navigation between parts
- Progress indicators
- Series overview pages

## Best Practices for Your Garden

### Start Small, Grow Organically

Don't wait for perfection. Plant small seeds of ideas and nurture them over time:

```markdown
## Initial Thought (Day 1)
Idea: Digital gardens vs blogs

## Expanded (Week 1)  
Gardens grow non-linearly, blogs are chronological...

## Refined (Month 1)
[Full developed article with examples and connections]
```

### Use Descriptive Links

Instead of generic "click here," use meaningful link text:

```markdown
❌ Learn more about [[this topic]] here
✅ Explore [[knowledge management principles]]
```

### Build Bidirectional Connections

When linking to another page, consider adding a reciprocal link back. This creates a rich knowledge web.

### Tag Thoughtfully

Use tags to create thematic clusters:
- Keep tags broad enough to group multiple pieces
- Use 2-5 tags per post for best results
- Consider creating tag landing pages with context

## The Garden Metaphor

Traditional blogs are like **assembly lines** - content flows in one direction, from newest to oldest.

Digital gardens are like **ecosystems** - ideas grow, branch, and cross-pollinate:

```
    🌱 Seedlings → 🌿 Budding → 🌳 Evergreen
    (notes)     (drafts)    (polished)
```

Some pages will stay as seedlings indefinitely - and that's perfectly fine! The goal is cultivation, not completion.

## Getting Started Today

1. **Install Vitis** and create your first post
2. **Add wiki-links** between 2-3 existing pages  
3. **Check the graph** to see connections forming
4. **Publish without perfection** - let your garden grow over time

Your digital garden is a living space. It will evolve with you as you learn, explore, and make new connections.
